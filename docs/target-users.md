# Target Users

## Primary Persona: Indonesian Home-based F&B Seller
**Profile:** 
- **Name:** Ibu Rini (Example)
- **Business:** Home bakery making brownies and dessert boxes.
- **Tech Savvy:** Low to Medium. Uses WhatsApp, Instagram, and basic mobile apps.
- **Needs:** Needs to know exactly how much it costs to make one box of brownies and what a safe selling price is to make a 40% profit.

## Secondary Personas
- **Small Cloud Kitchen Owners:** Need to quickly price new menu items.
- **Resellers/Dropshippers:** Need to calculate margins after buying from suppliers and adding packaging/shipping costs.
- **Catering Beginners:** Need to estimate per-pax pricing based on raw material fluctuations.

## User Pain Points
- Confused by complex financial terms (COGS, Overhead, Depreciation).
- Unsure if they are actually making money or just covering costs.
- Forgets how they priced an item months ago.
- Overwhelmed by Microsoft Excel or complex POS/accounting software.
- Afraid of pricing too high and losing customers, or too low and losing money.

## User Goals
- Quickly find out the cost to produce one unit of their product.
- Know the exact profit per unit.
- Get reassurance that their selling price is "safe" and profitable.
- Keep a simple record of their calculations.

## User Fears & Confusions
- **Fear of Math:** Intimidated by long formulas.
- **Confusion on Margins:** Doesn't understand the difference between markup and margin.
- **Fear of Loss:** Afraid that hidden costs (like packaging or electricity) are eating into their profits.

## User Skill Level Assumptions
- **Financial literacy:** Low. They know basic addition/subtraction but lack accounting concepts.
- **Technical literacy:** Mobile-first. Comfortable with simple forms but not complex dashboards.

## How the App Should Speak to Them
The app must act as a friendly, supportive assistant. 
- Use encouraging words ("Great! Your business is healthy!").
- Avoid jargon ("Margin" can be used but needs simple context, avoid "EBITDA" or "COGS").
- Use familiar terms ("Modal Produksi" instead of "Total Expenses").
